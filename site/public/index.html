Hakita 09.02.2019
The variety on display here is great. A lot of the songs are meh but since they're all pretty short it won't take long until you're on something new and fresh. The post-rock and ambient tracks were by far my favorite, such as Slum and Backtrack Observation, though a lot of banger tracks like How Is That Legal and Press and Turn were great too. The only real downsides of this album are the fact that, since it's 38 songs, you're bound to not even remember half of them by the time you're done and a couple awkward moments of attempts at comedy. Regardless, it's a good album with lots of relisten value and charm. It's the kinda album that's easy to just put on and let flow, making it pretty accessible. Despite being long, I was never once bored during it, and will probably relisten to it in the future.
1Chops 01.01.2018
Haven't heard the whole thing yet, but if the batting average keeps up over the whole project, this is the first really good album of 2o18. It's so rare that I can get excited about instrumental rock, but the atmosphere Naked Flames created is so dead-on perfect that I can't help but get drawn in. Defo feels like some kinda dream diary. I guess my only real complaint is that some of the longer songs get kinda meandery/lose me a little as they stretch onward ("Secretweapon" really does this in that first half). And the absence of a human voice really goes noticed sometimes. Other than that THESE TUNES ARE GOOD and I'm excited to finish off the tracklist.
